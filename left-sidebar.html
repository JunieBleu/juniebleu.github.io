My Whiteboards

In my classroom, Students will always come in to a whiteboard loaded with content. Part of my teaching habits is to have everything up on the board, including: Learning Outcomes, Lesson aims, Steop-by-step of the lesson, Date of lesson, reminders , tips & tricks and samples of work (usually samples I have made, to display my skill and knowledge in the area).

My Teaching Philosophy

As an art teacher, my primary goal is to ignite a passion for creativity in my students and immerse them in the vibrant world of art. I believe that every student has the potential to be an artist, and my role is to guide them in discovering and nurturing their unique creative voices.

I strive to create a classroom environment where students are actively engaged and involved in the creative process. By introducing a variety of artistic mediums and techniques, I encourage students to explore and experiment, fostering a sense of curiosity and excitement about art.

My teaching approach is rooted in inquiry-based learning, where students are encouraged to ask questions, investigate, and reflect on their artistic choices. This method not only enhances their critical thinking skills but also allows them to take ownership of their learning journey. Through guided exploration and open-ended projects, students learn to think deeply about their work and the world around them.

I employ consequence and action classroom management methods to maintain a positive and productive learning environment. Clear expectations and consistent consequences help students understand the impact of their actions, promoting responsibility and respect. By addressing issues promptly and fairly, I create a safe space where students feel valued and supported.
